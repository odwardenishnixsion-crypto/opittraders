Introduction
Historical Back ground Opit Traders Saving and Credit Network Loan Association is a profit-oriented association which started as a small enterprise group comprising of a few members who were businessmen and women dealing in produce,and at the same time they were practicing farming at small scale and majorly produce products. This was in the year 2011, 31, July when the association started. It started with 15 members comprising of male and female. The group have come to know the importance of being in the network, that it would be of economic andsocial development.To focus on these effectively, they needed a collective effort on basis of poverty eradication, self-reliance, social infrastructure and business development. The group members agreed to contribute a share of 0ne million (1,000,000=) per person at the being, registration fee of ten thousand shillings (10,000=) and three thousand (3,000=) monthly welfare. The creation of these pool of fund was primarily to generate fund for lending to a qualified member as a loan. Since 2011 the number of members joining the group has been rapidly increasing. Currently the association has over 250 active members who are both business persons, civil servants, and famers within the community. Opit Traders Saving and Credit Network, LoanAssociation is currently located at, Opit Upper Cell, Opit Central Ward, Omoro Town Council. Omoro District, Northern Uganda.

Activities done by the OTSCNW
There are two broad activities carried out by the associations that is; Loan; The association has two types of loans interest that is 15% for non-members and 10% for shareholders in a period of six months and ten months respectively. The loan principle is given to the client depending on the security pledged. Normally the most preferred security is land and motorcycle or vehicles where the document presented are land sale agreement and log book respectively. Additionally, photos of a pledged collaterals and the business are printed and put in the file for more evidence. They also consider the two guarantors’ consent. The first should be immediate person (husband or wife) and the second guarantor should be a person with the share or having an account with the association. Normally priority is given to shareholders. Types of loan The most preferred are business loans, motorcycle loans and agricultural loan they normally deal in agricultural equipment and tools. To the smaller scale the loans are given for school fees loans. Savings Associations requires members to save in two ways; Firstly, we have volunteering saving, this is where the member saves on his or her own will and there is compulsory saving where 10% is charged on loan principle borrowed. Share sailing Share is predominately done by the board of directors and also the person whom is supposed to buy. The executive board of directors normally consider the following the truck record of the client for the past two years that is how he had been Managing the loan, the level of cooperation with other members, honesty and the capability of a person. Farming The association has the farm land where they grow crops (maize, sorghum, soya) for commercial purpose. And its members are engaged in the commercial farming and they have the capacity of bulk produce. Market The association has a committee who look for market for association produce and for the members as well. Challenges Farm challenges; Expensive farm equipment’s and tools; association uses a lot of money (fund) in hiring farm equipment’s like tractor for instances to plough 40 acres requires 4million per ploughing also other farm equipment like sprayer, thrusting machine and tool farms like hand holes, slashes. Lack of transport like truck to transport the produce from the field to the store as well as to the market. Lack of enough labor force; man power has been a problem during planting, harvesting period. The association was also facing a challenge of counterfeit Agroinput. Some of the Agro input are substandard. Like the pest sides, poor quality seeds. Lack of harvestingequipment’slike tubulins, bagging, combine harvesters. Maize thrusters’ machines. Lack of storage facilities the association has no storage facility which sometimes forces the sale of the products at a low price due to high cost of rent and store space. Climatic change; change in whether conditions that is to say unreliable rainfall, as lead to low yield/production of products giving losses to the association. Additionally, the association uses some Agro chemicals to protect the crops from strong sunshine. Pest and diseases; this has been a problem in that to control pest and diseases the association uses a lot of money to buy Agro chemicals like pest sides. Hence high cost of productions. Low price of agricultural produce especially during harvesting season which force association to store the produce so that it waits for the season when the price has shot up yet association keep meeting the cost of renting a store. Financial challenges The emergency of COVID 19 in 2020 to 2022, our clients who had taken loans failed to pay back and the clients applying for loan reduced thus forcing the association to undergo a financial loss Lack of office equipment like safe, strong cupboards, office tables, office chairs. The association depends on manual work which are very tiresome and slow, making serving a client difficult and time wasting compared to computer usage. Lack of sufficient liquidity and low risk management, the association is sometimes over whelmed by customers (borrowers) which results into poor credit worthiness and loss of members confidence since the money for loan is not enough. Leadership challenges; the leaders of the associations lack leadership skill and knowledge especially in human resource management. Poor record keeping due to lack of knowledge and accounting principles. normally the office bearer has limited knowledge on classifying different accounts and book keeping which makes auditing difficult and time consuming to make the report. Poor loan recovery; some clients’ needs follow up right from their homes in order to recover the loan or the security. this sometimes demand involvement of more members to go for operations which is expensive in term of transport because association has no transport means so they base on hiring motorcycle.
